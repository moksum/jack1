The photograph of the physicists who participated in the 5th Solvay Conference has been described as the most intelligent gathering of people in history. 17 of the 29 individuals were recipients of the Nobel Prize, and included such household names as Einstein, Bohr, Curie, Planck, and Heisenberg. So striking was the richness of the company that the photo was equated with an anachronistic gathering of Archimedes, Kepler, Newton, and Galileo. Often overshadowed by the photo, is the profound impact of the few papers that were actually presented. More on the photo. a link.

Conference Schedule

right column

Monday 24 October: W.L. Bragg's report, followed by discussion.

Tuesday 25 October, started 9:00 a.m.: reception.

Tuesday 25 October, afternoon: L. deBroglie's report, followed by discussion.

Wednesday 26 October, morning: M. Born and W.Heisenberg's report, followed by discussion.

Wednesday 26 October, afternoon: E. Schrodinger's report, followed by discussion.

Thursday 27 October, morning: general discussion (Bohr's contribution per discussion.)

Friday 28 October, trip to Paris for procession at the Sorbonne.

Saturday 29 October: lunch with the King and Queen of the Belgians.
